Proxy Flow
  1. Consumer opens up Client Website and ShieldConex® iframe elements are loaded into the Consumer’s Browser.
  2. As Consumer enters data into the iframe elements, they are captured directly by ShieldConex® and tokenized.
  3. A BFID (Bluefin ID) is returned.
  4. Tokens are cached in ShieldConex® for a Maximum of 1 hour.
  5. All data needed to complete the transaction is sent to the Clients processing service, including the BFID.
  6. Using the BFID, the Client requests their tokens and they are returned.
  7. The Client sends the message to ShieldConex® in the format required by their processor with the tokens in place rather than PAN (credit card).
  8. The ShieldConex® Proxy will detokenize the tokens, reinsert the actual PAN data into the payload, and relay the payment request to the processor.
  9. The response is proxied back to the Client.

Detokenization Flow

  1. The Client sends the message to ShieldConex® in the format required by their processor with the tokens in place rather than PAN (credit card).
  2. The ShieldConex® Proxy will detokenize the tokens, reinsert the actual PAN data into the payload, and relay the payment request to the processor.

Tokenization Flow

  1. Consumer opens up Client Website and ShieldConex® iframe elements are loaded into the Consumer’s Browser.
  2. As Consumer enters data into the iframe elements, they are captured directly by ShieldConex® and tokenized.
  3. A BFID (Bluefin ID) is returned.
  4. Tokens are cached in ShieldConex® for a Maximum of 1 hour.
  5. All data needed to complete the transaction is sent to the Clients processing service, including the BFID.
